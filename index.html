Principal R & D Engineer — High-Temperature H2 Reactors

The Principal R & D Engineer (Hi-T H2 Reactors) will contribute to the design and development of a novel high-temperature process for hydrogen ironmaking in a major R & D project at the Robinson Research Institute. Steelmaking is New Zealand’s largest single source of industrial CO2 emissions, but these could ultimately be eliminated through commercialisation of our ‘hydrogen-DRI’ process. Today, we are at the start of our scale-up journey and this role is a key part of this exciting opportunity for change. As an experienced industrial process engineer in this growing team, you will be working with us to change the future of industrial steelmaking in New Zealand, and around the world.
